Projects - Old and New

Emberlite

September 2023

Emberlite is a web app developed by my team during the Pitt Challenge 2023, a medical themed hackathon. Our web app aims to improve mental health in medical professions and combat burnout in medical workers. We utilize a Python backend tied to a MySQL server in order to recieve real time inputs from the app, and to store and visualize data. Our front end is composed of CSS/HTML, and our web app was locally hosted. The project was completed in a 42 hour timeframe, and we recieved first place for our track "Burnout in Healthcare Workers".

See it in action with our Devpost here!

Micromouse

Feburary 2023 - Current

Micromouse is a national competition for STEM students to create an autonomous robot capable of traversing a maze. As a member of the navigation and movement teams at Pitt, my responsibilities include creating C++ based navigation algorithms for traversing and solving the maze, and creating PID control systems for our robot using inputs from infrared and sonar sensors. Our team plans to compete in the regional competition in Pittsburgh in Spring 2024.

Learn more about Micro-mouse here!

Train Model

August 2023 - Current

This project is based in fundemental software design. As a group of 7, my teammates and I are tasked with designing and implementing a realistic simulation software of our local train system, with our professor acting as the client. We are taught to employ agile design methods, such as weekly scrum meetings, vast documentation, customer collaboration, and weekly task delegation. This project is meant to prepare students to develop software as a team, and throughout the semester I will gain invaluable skills that will translate well into industry.
